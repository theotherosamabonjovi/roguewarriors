Modern Tactical Skirmish — Digital Edition

Based on Rogue Warriors: A Modern Warfare Skirmish Game

📖 How to Play

🎯 Objective

Eliminate all enemy units. Each player commands a Fire Team of 4–8 specialists across a tactical battlefield. Last team standing wins.

⚡ Turn Structure — Alternate Activations

  • Players alternate activating one unit at a time.
  • Each activated unit gets 2 Actions.
  • Once all units have been activated, a new Round begins.
  • Round start: suppression clears, overwatch resets.

👣 Actions

  • Move (1 action): Move up to your Move value in tiles. Click a blue tile.
  • Sprint (2 actions): Move up to 2× your Move value. Cannot shoot this activation.
  • Shoot (1 action): Fire at an enemy in range & line of sight. Click a red enemy.
  • Aim (1 action): +1 to hit on your next Shoot this activation.
  • Take Cover (1 action): Gain +1 Defense until your next activation.
  • Charge (2 actions): Melee attack on an adjacent enemy.
  • Ability (1–2 actions): Use your unit's specialist ability.

🎲 Combat — Shooting

  • Roll attack dice (1d6 per die shown). Hit on your Shoot Skill or higher.
  • Short range: no penalty. Medium: +1 to target. Long: +2 to target.
  • Each hit: defender rolls 1d6 vs their Defense value. Equal or higher = saved.
  • Failed save = wound (unit eliminated). Near-miss = suppression (−1 to next roll).
  • Cover: +1 to Defense saves (tile & active cover stacks, max +2).

🦸 Specialists

  • Rifleman: Balanced all-rounder. 1 attack die.
  • Sniper: Long range, high accuracy, Overwatch — watch a zone for 2 turns.
  • Medic: Can Heal one adjacent ally per game (remove suppression or revive).
  • Grenadier: 2 attack dice, short range, Blast hits target + adjacent tiles.
  • Leader: Command Aura — allies within 3 tiles may reroll one failed die per attack.
  • Scout: Fast (Move 6), Stealth — enemies need 6+ to wound while in cover.

🌐 Multiplayer

Use Online Multiplayer to play with friends. Player 1 hosts and shares their Room Code. Player 2 enters it to connect. No account needed — peer-to-peer via the browser.
